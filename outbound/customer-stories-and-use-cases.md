## Cotiss — Procurement SaaS, Auckland NZ

**Company profile:** Procurement software startup, Series B, expanding into AU/NZ. 11–50 employees.
**Buyer:** GTM/Ops — owns all prospecting and enrichment. SDRs are phone-only.
**Prior tools:** Apollo, Lusha, and other global tools with weak AU/NZ coverage.

**Results:**
- Contact accuracy: ~30% → **85–90%** with Firmable
- Call connects: **more than doubled within weeks**
- Onboarding: new reps productive within **30 minutes**

**Best for:** SDR Manager, Sales Leader, RevOps personas. Any prospect struggling with AU/NZ data quality or global tool limitations.

**Key quotes:**
- "Every other database hasn't focused on Australia at all"
- "You've just completely opened up my world with the fact that it's one credit for all of the information"
- "We've been struggling so much for A&Z data"
- "Stabbing in the dark" — couldn't optimise their waterfall because coverage varied so much

**Why they switched:**
- Current enrichment cost ~$1.50 per contact (5+ waterfall steps with global tools)
- Firmable: one credit (~33 cents) = name, LinkedIn, mobile, email, company data — all in one hit

**Standout features they valued:**
- Verified AU/NZ mobile numbers (SDRs are phone-first)
- One credit = full profile (no multi-step waterfall)
- Local government/registry data (aged care, local council, state gov)

## Stripe — B2B Platform, ANZ/APAC

**Company profile:** B2B company targeting retail, insurance, travel, and tech verticals across ANZ/APAC. Mid-market (dedicated marketing ops, separate AEs, Salesforce + Marketo stack).
**Buyer:** Marketing lead (Jayne), Data/Marketing Ops (Ian), multiple stakeholders.
**Prior situation:** Data flowed in indirectly via sales scraping ZoomInfo — marketing had no direct sourcing tool.

**Results from evaluation:**
- Platforms vertical: 282/312 companies matched (90%)
- Retail: 77/82 (94%)
- Insurance: 44/44 (100%)
- Mobile coverage example: 14/15 mobile numbers for Specsavers contacts

**Best for:** RevOps / Marketing Ops persona. Prospects who want to own prospecting (not depend on sales for data), or who need ABM list building against named accounts.

**Key message:**
- "We don't want to wait for things to happen — we want to be directly involved in acquiring"
- Marketing should own prospecting, not wait for sales to bring contacts in

**Why they evaluated Firmable:**
- Marketing wanted direct data sourcing (not via sales scraping ZoomInfo)
- Building lists against defined must-win accounts by vertical
- Needed suppression list upload to avoid re-targeting existing customers
