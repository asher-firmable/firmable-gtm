# Firmable: APAC's AI-native challenger to ZoomInfo

Firmable is a Melbourne-based B2B sales intelligence platform purpose-built for Australia, New Zealand, and the broader APAC market. Founded in 2023 by the team behind **Aconex** (acquired by Oracle for **$1.6 billion**), the company has raised **$23 million AUD** across seed and Series A rounds led by Airtree Ventures, and now serves **1,000+ paying customers** including CBRE, Monday.com, Canon, DHL, Robert Half, and G2. Its core thesis is simple but potent: the dominant US-built sales intelligence tools (ZoomInfo, Apollo, Lusha) have poor data coverage outside North America, and APAC sales teams deserve a platform built natively for their markets. With a **4.7/5 star rating on G2** across 65 reviews and a March 2026 Series A earmarked for US expansion, Firmable is transitioning from regional disruptor to global contender.

---

## What Firmable actually does

Firmable is an **AI-powered B2B data and sales intelligence platform** that combines a proprietary database with real-time buying signals and AI agents to help sales teams find, time, and close deals. The platform operates across three layers:

**Proprietary data assembly.** Firmable aggregates data from hundreds of sources, then uses AI web crawling, LLM-based extraction, and entity resolution to build and continuously refresh a database of **3 million+ verified company profiles** and **25 million+ decision-maker contacts** with over **1,000 data attributes**. These include firmographics (industry, revenue, employee count, location), contact data (verified mobile numbers, work emails, LinkedIn profiles), technographics (tech stack), and APAC-specific attributes like ABN/NZBN business identifiers and Australian Do Not Call Register flags.

**Buying signals engine.** AI agents continuously monitor target accounts for signals such as leadership changes, funding rounds, hiring surges, company expansions, technology adoption changes, and — as of January 2026 — **search intent data** showing which companies are actively researching relevant topics. These signals are surfaced as daily alerts tied directly to CRM accounts, enabling reps to time outreach to moments of maximum receptivity.

**AI-powered workflows.** The platform enables natural-language ICP definition (describe your ideal customer in plain English), automatic prospect list generation, AI-generated account summaries before calls, and CRM enrichment agents that keep Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Microsoft Dynamics data fresh without manual work. A Chrome/Edge browser extension surfaces contact details and company intelligence while browsing LinkedIn or any website.

## Who Firmable sells to

Firmable targets **B2B companies operating in or selling into the APAC region**, with its strongest foothold in Australia and New Zealand. The ICP spans multiple dimensions:

**By role**, the platform has dedicated solution pages for six personas: sales leaders (pipeline visibility, territory mapping), account executives (deal intelligence, account context), SDRs (list building, connection rates), revenue operations (CRM accuracy, data hygiene), marketing teams (ABM, lead enrichment, campaign targeting), and recruiters (talent sourcing, business development lists). The heaviest messaging investment is on sales leadership and SDR use cases.

**By company size**, pricing tiers range from an Individual plan at **US$55/month** (single user, 1,200 profiles/year) through to custom Enterprise plans at **US$170+/month** with API access, SSO, and dedicated account management. The self-serve free trial funnel targets SMBs and mid-market, while the sales-led Enterprise motion serves larger organizations. Named customers range from startups like ProcurePro and MyCISO to global enterprises like CBRE, DHL, Canon, and Marsh — suggesting traction across the spectrum.

**By industry**, G2 reviewer profiles and case studies indicate strongest adoption in technology/SaaS, consulting, construction, recruitment, financial services, and professional services. The platform's data attributes (industry codes, tech stack, hiring signals) naturally favor B2B-focused verticals.

**By geography**, Firmable has localized product versions for Australia, New Zealand, Malaysia, Singapore, and the United States, with the March 2026 Series A earmarking funds for deeper US market entry. The company claims coverage across **eight APAC markets**.

## Competitive advantages and how Firmable stacks up

Firmable competes against a crowded field — ZoomInfo, Apollo.io, Lusha, Cognism, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, and Demandbase — but occupies a distinct niche. Its competitive moat rests on four pillars:

**Proprietary APAC data asset.** While most sales intelligence startups license third-party data, Firmable builds its own dataset from scratch using AI-powered web aggregation, LLM extraction, and manual verification. Airtree partner John Henderson highlighted this in the Series A announcement: *"Firmable is one of very few companies globally that owns its own proprietary dataset and is building agents directly on top of it."* This is particularly defensible in APAC, where data is harder to aggregate than in the US due to fragmented sources, different business registration systems, and less standardized digital footprints.

**APAC-native features global tools can't replicate.** These include ABN/NZBN business identifier mapping, Australian DNC Register integration, local mobile number formats, region-specific industry classifications, and entity resolution connecting franchises and company divisions common in ANZ markets. No global competitor offers this depth of local contextualization.

**Pricing and packaging flexibility.** ZoomInfo's enterprise-only pricing and annual lock-in contracts are a well-known pain point. Firmable offers **14-day free trials, monthly billing, no lock-in contracts**, and transparent per-user pricing starting at US$55/month. This makes it accessible to startups and SMBs that ZoomInfo's minimum contracts exclude. The company also offers not-for-profit discounts.

**AI-native architecture.** Unlike incumbents that bolted AI features onto legacy database products, Firmable was built from inception (2023) as an AI-native platform. Its AI agents handle prospect discovery, signal monitoring, CRM enrichment, and account intelligence — positioning it as a next-generation platform rather than a database with search filters.

## Data coverage in detail

Firmable's database encompasses **3 million+ company profiles** and **25 million+ contacts** across APAC, with the deepest coverage in Australia and New Zealand. Their Capterra listing references **1 million+ Australian B2B companies and 10 million people** specifically, suggesting the broader 25 million figure includes APAC-wide expansion.

**Company data** includes industry classification, revenue, employee count (local and global headcount), location, digital presence metrics (web traffic, social followers, reviews), ABN/NZBN identifiers, and entity resolution linking parent companies, subsidiaries, and franchises. **Contact data** spans verified mobile numbers, work email addresses, job titles, seniority levels, LinkedIn profiles, and inferred gender. **Technographic data** covers technology stack adoption and changes. **Signal data** includes leadership changes, hiring activity, funding rounds, company milestones, technology shifts, and — since January 2026 — **search intent** with company-level surge ratings, weekly trend indicators, and topic-level insights.

The data quality pipeline involves aggregation from hundreds of sources, AI-powered cleansing (deduplication, normalization), manual verification, and continuous refresh. The company emphasizes **entity resolution** as a particular strength — connecting related organizations like franchises and divisions — which is especially relevant in Australia's franchise-heavy economy.

Geographic coverage currently spans eight APAC markets with localized product versions for Australia, New Zealand, Malaysia, Singapore, and the United States. The US market represents the expansion frontier funded by the March 2026 Series A.

## What customers and reviewers actually say

The G2 review corpus (65 reviews, **4.7/5 stars**) reveals consistent patterns. The most frequently praised attributes are **data accuracy** (34 mentions), **contact information quality** (34 mentions), **ease of use** (28 mentions), and **customer support** (25 mentions). The most common criticisms relate to occasional data inaccuracies (9 mentions), some outdated records (8 mentions), and missing features or information for niche industries (8 mentions).

The most commercially compelling testimonial comes from an unnamed G2 reviewer who reported: *"Firmable has enabled us to generate over $2 million in pipeline from outbound activities in just a few short months."* Firmable's own SDR team claims **$160,000 in monthly pipeline per SDR** with a **15–20% call-to-meeting booking rate** — metrics they use as social proof in their content marketing.

The Public Sector Network's David Pierotti provided a direct competitive benchmark: *"Against other platforms, Firmable delivered accurate contact details for twice as many contacts, proving its superior value."* This two-to-one data advantage claim, while from a single customer, aligns with the broader pattern of reviewers reporting that ANZ data from Firmable significantly outperforms global alternatives.

Negative reviews cluster around data gaps in **niche or non-standard industries** (one sports-industry user noted difficulty finding contacts at sporting clubs) and occasional outdated records — challenges inherent to any B2B database. Firmable actively responds to negative G2 reviews with offers to help, suggesting strong customer success investment.

## Recent developments signal aggressive expansion

Firmable's trajectory over the past 12 months reveals a company accelerating on multiple fronts. The **$14 million Series A** closed in March 2026 — led by Airtree Ventures with participation from notable Australian tech figures including former Pacific Brands CEO Sue Morphet and A Cloud Guru cofounder Sam Kroonenburg — is earmarked for three priorities: US market expansion, AI agent development, and specialist sales data.

The **January 2026 launch of search intent signals** represents Firmable's move into the high-value intent data space previously dominated by Bombora, 6sense, and Demandbase. This add-on (available on Enterprise plans) provides company-level surge ratings, weekly trend indicators, and topic-level buying intent aligned to customer ICPs.

Earlier milestones include the **New Zealand launch** (May 2025), **Salesforce integration** (November 2024), and reaching **1,500 users from 500+ companies** by December 2024 — just nine months after general availability. The team has grown to approximately **45–60 people**, and the company now operates across eight APAC markets.

The US expansion plan is particularly noteworthy. Rather than competing head-to-head with ZoomInfo on US data, Firmable appears to be targeting **APAC-headquartered or APAC-selling companies in the US** and building outward from there — a wedge strategy that mirrors Aconex's own international expansion playbook that ultimately led to its billion-dollar acquisition by Oracle.

## Conclusion

Firmable has built a defensible position as the **leading APAC-native B2B sales intelligence platform** by combining proprietary local data, AI-native architecture, and founder credibility from the Aconex era. Its competitive moat is not just data coverage — it's the combination of APAC-specific compliance features (DNC, ABN), entity resolution tuned for local business structures, verified mobile numbers that materially improve connection rates, and pricing that undercuts global incumbents by an order of magnitude.

The key risk is whether the APAC data advantage translates to other markets as the company expands into the US. The key opportunity is timing: with AI transforming sales workflows and global incumbents struggling with international data quality, Firmable occupies a rare position as both a data company and an AI-native platform — the combination investors increasingly value most. The Airtree-backed Series A and the founding team's track record of scaling an Australian B2B company to a $1.6 billion exit suggest the ambition and execution capability to pursue that opportunity aggressively.
