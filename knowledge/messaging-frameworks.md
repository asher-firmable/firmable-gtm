# Messaging Frameworks

> Summary of email copywriting frameworks and persona→template routing. Used by email-copywriting skill and /generate-copy command. Update whenever you discover messaging that works or doesn't. Full examples are in `projects/outbound/email-templates-examples.md`.

---

## Core Principles (always apply)

1. **Lead with their pain, not our product.** Never open with "Firmable is..."
2. **Specificity wins.** "Your SDR team at [company]" beats "sales teams like yours."
3. **Short and scannable.** Max 100 words for cold emails. 3–4 sentences.
4. **One CTA only.**
5. **No corporate fluff.** Banned words: "leverage", "synergy", "best-in-class", "industry-leading", "cutting-edge."
6. **Never start with "I" or "We."**
7. **Match tone to segment:** Enterprise = formal/direct | Mid-market = conversational | SMB = very casual

## Framework Selection

| Situation | Framework to use |
|---|---|
| Specific signal available (hiring, funding, expansion, new leader) | Signal-based (PVP or PQS) |
| No signal, but clear ICP fit | Pain-led (PQS) |
| Known competitor tech detected (ZoomInfo, Apollo, Lusha, Cognism) | Competitive displacement |
| Want to test core value prop cleanly | Direct Email |
| Early-stage curiosity, low-friction approach | Trojan Horse |
| Have a compelling free offer (audit, sample list) | Audit Offer |

---

## Frameworks

### 1. Signal-based (PVP / PQS) — highest performing
Use when: We have a specific trigger (job posting, funding, expansion, tech change, leadership hire).

```
Hi [First Name],

Noticed [specific signal — e.g., "you're hiring 3 SDRs in Sydney"].

Usually when [company type] scales outbound like that, [pain point — e.g., "the biggest bottleneck is finding enough qualified APAC accounts"].

We help [similar companies] [specific outcome — e.g., "source 3x more qualified accounts without buying stale lists"].

Worth a quick chat this week?
```

### 2. Pain-led (PQS) — primary template for general outbound
Use when: No specific signal, but the prospect matches ICP.

```
Hi [First Name],

Quick question — [pain-point question relevant to their role]

[One sentence on how Firmable solves it, tailored to their segment.]

[Social proof — e.g., "Teams like Cotiss doubled call connects within weeks."]

Open to a 15-min call?
```

### 3. Competitive displacement
Use when: We know or suspect they use ZoomInfo, Apollo, Lusha, or Cognism.

```
Hi [First Name],

Noticed [company] is using [competitor].

Most teams using [competitor] for APAC outreach find the same thing: mobile numbers don't connect, emails bounce, and AU/NZ contacts are thin. Those platforms weren't built for this region.

We cover 3M+ APAC companies with verified local mobiles — 22% connect rate vs the ~5% you're probably seeing now.

Worth a quick look at what we'd find for your ICP?
```

### 4. Direct Email
Use when: Clean value prop test, smaller businesses, or very senior buyers.

```
Hi [First Name],

If I could help you [specific outcome] in [timeframe], like we did for [company], would you be open to a conversation?
```

### 5. Audit Offer
Use when: Compelling free offer available (sample list, data coverage comparison).

```
Hi [First Name],

We're offering a free APAC coverage comparison — we'll pull 50 contacts from your ICP and show you what your current tool is missing vs. Firmable.

Reply "yes" and I'll send the link.

P.S. Takes 5 minutes and you keep the data either way.
```

### 6. Trojan Horse
Use when: Low-friction entry, curious persona, not yet problem-aware.

Email 1: `[Name] — do you still [core thing they do]?`
Email 2: Deliver value, introduce Firmable after they reply.

## Persona → Template Routing

| Persona | Primary trigger | Recommended framework |
|---|---|---|
| SDR Manager | Competitor tech detected | Competitive displacement |
| SDR Manager | Hiring SDRs | Signal-based (PQS) |
| SDR Manager | No signal | Pain-led (connect rate angle) |
| RevOps / Marketing Ops | No signal | PQS (deliverability / DNC angle) |
| RevOps / Marketing Ops | Named account list | Audit Offer |
| Sales Leader | Pipeline pain | PQS or Direct Email |
| Sales Leader | Competitor tech | Competitive displacement |
| Recruitment Consultant | No signal | Trojan Horse or PQS |
| Recruitment Consultant | BD/hiring signals | Signal-based |

---

## Subject Line Patterns

1. Signal-reference: `[Company] + [signal]`
2. Pain-as-question: `Australian contact data?`
3. Competitor name-drop: `[Competitor] + APAC coverage`
4. Short and curious: `Quick question, [First Name]`
5. Result reference: `How Cotiss doubled cold call connects`
