## Primary Competitors

### ZoomInfo
- **Strengths**: Massive US database, strong brand, deep integrations, well-known
- **Weaknesses**: APAC data is thin and often stale. Expensive ($15K–30K+/year team minimum). Annual lock-in contracts. Not DNC-compliant for Australia.
- **Firmable advantage**: 5–10x better Australian/NZ contact coverage and freshness. Fraction of the cost. Monthly billing, no lock-in. DNC built in.
- **Displacement angle**: "Your US data is fine — but how's your APAC coverage?"
- **Objection handle**: "How's your APAC data quality? We often complement ZoomInfo for teams selling into AU/NZ."

### Apollo.io
- **Strengths**: Good UI, affordable, strong email sequencing built-in, free tier
- **Weaknesses**: APAC data quality is mediocre. Scrapes heavily — compliance risk in AU (no DNC compliance). Limited local mobile numbers.
- **Firmable advantage**: Data quality and DNC compliance for APAC. Verified local mobile numbers vs. scraped data.
- **Displacement angle**: "How many bounced emails from your AU lists last quarter?"
- **Objection handle**: "For US data, Apollo is fine. But check your bounce rates on AU contacts."

### Lusha
- **Strengths**: Easy to use, Chrome extension, good for individual reps, free tier
- **Weaknesses**: Limited APAC data. Point solution for individuals, not teams. Multiple credits per contact. No DNC compliance.
- **Firmable advantage**: Full platform vs. point solution. Team workflows. One credit = full profile. DNC built in.
- **Displacement angle**: "How many mobile numbers actually connect when you're calling AU prospects?"

### Cognism
- **Strengths**: Strong in Europe (especially GDPR compliance), good intent data
- **Weaknesses**: APAC coverage is limited — built for European and US markets. Expensive.
- **Firmable advantage**: APAC-native data. Same compliance story but for Australian market (DNC Register).
- **Displacement angle**: "Cognism is great for Europe — but they weren't built for the Australian market."

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- **Strengths**: Most professionals are on LinkedIn. Good for research and account mapping. Widely trusted.
- **Weaknesses**: No direct email or mobile data. No API for bulk ops. Manual and time-consuming. Can't scale.
- **Firmable advantage**: Actionable contact data at scale, API access, CRM sync, verified mobiles.
- **Displacement angle**: "Great for research — but how do you scale to 1,000+ contacts with verified emails and mobiles?"

## Competitive Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already use ZoomInfo" | "How's your APAC data quality? We often run alongside ZoomInfo for teams selling into AU/NZ." |
| "Apollo is cheaper" | "For US data, sure. But check your bounce rates on AU contacts. Apollo wasn't built for this region." |
| "We just use LinkedIn" | "Great for research. But how do you scale to 1,000+ contacts with verified emails and mobiles?" |
| "We're locked in for 12 months" | "No rush. Let's benchmark your APAC data coverage now so you have comparison data at renewal." |

## Disqualification Signal

If a prospect is locked into a multi-year ZoomInfo or Cognism contract, note it as a `C` tier account (marginal) and flag for re-engagement at renewal. Don't disqualify entirely — start a slow nurture now.
