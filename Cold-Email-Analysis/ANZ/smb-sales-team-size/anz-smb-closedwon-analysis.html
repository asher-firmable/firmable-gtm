Implications for Positioning & Messaging
Drawn from the ANZ-native cohort only (~96 companies).
What's Landing
  • 1
    "ANZ-native" is the core claim — it's not just a feature, it's the reason they're in the room. Lead with it every time.
  • 2
    The Apollo comparison is ready-made — customers bring it up unprompted. Apollo's ANZ data = scraped LinkedIn = no net-new contacts. Clean, provable displacement angle.
  • 3
    JobAdder integration is a recruitment-vertical acquisition moat — 4 named deals where it was decisive. Worth a standalone recruitment landing page and sequence.
  • 4
    Direct mobile numbers are primary value for cold callers and field sales — a distinct persona from the "data quality" buyer; responds to different copy entirely.
  • 5
    The "first outbound motion" buyer is under-served — they're not comparing tools, they're deciding whether to invest in outbound at all. Firmable needs messaging for this context.
Gaps to Investigate
  • !
    Discovery channel unknown for ~90% of deals — better tagging in Fathom notes or a HubSpot deal source field is needed immediately.
  • !
    "How did you hear about us?" is missing from most calls — should be a standard call-opening question with a structured Fathom capture field.
  • !
    Self-serve / no-recording cohort is invisible — ~66% of 300 deals have no Fathom recording. Understanding their path requires an in-product survey or post-signup email.
  • ?
    Non-ANZ HQ companies (6) — represent a different motion (global mandate + local data gap). Worth a separate ICP track: "global company with ANZ team."
