# US Competitor Displacement — ZoomInfo

Two-email sequence targeting US-based startups and mid-market companies where ZoomInfo is detected in the tech stack. Email 1 drives a demo booking; Email 2 drives a trial.

---

## Campaign Overview

- **Target**: US B2B companies (startups + mid-market) using ZoomInfo — detected via Firmable technographics or self-reported
- **Sequence**: 2 emails
- **Email 1 CTA**: Book a demo (20-minute call)
- **Email 2 CTA**: Start a trial (self-serve link)
- **Tone**: Direct, conversational, US English — no corporate filler
- **Personas**: growth_leader, sdr_manager, bizdev_leader, channel_partner, revops, sales_leader

---

## Competitive Angles

Four angles. Use them as context when reviewing copy — they inform the fixed email template body.

### 1. Built-in signals, not add-ons
ZoomInfo charges separately for intent signals, Copilot, and buyer alerts — each is a separate module, a separate contract line. Firmable bundles signals into the core platform at no extra cost.

### 2. Flat renewal rates
ZoomInfo is known for renewal price hikes — what you signed is rarely what you pay the following year. Firmable's renewal rate does not move. No renegotiation, no surprises.

### 3. Built for your size, not enterprise
ZoomInfo is architected for 500+ seat orgs with dedicated ops support. Startups and mid-market teams end up paying enterprise complexity tax without getting enterprise leverage.

### 4. Early US roadmap access (PS line — required)
Firmable is in early US rollout. First US accounts get direct access to the team and a seat at roadmap sessions. This goes in the PS of both emails. Not optional.

## Firmable Signals Library

**People signals:**
- New joiner / new in role — first 90 days is typically the peak vendor evaluation window
- Role change — shift in mandate, often open to new tools
- Leaver — contact just left a company
- Follow contacts to new companies — track contacts as they move between orgs

**Company signals:**
- New c-suite appointment / leadership change
- Company raising funds
- Actively hiring in sales
- Actively hiring in engineering
- Hiring surge in a specific function
- New product launch
- Market expansion — entering a new geography or vertical
- M&A activity
- Financial growth

- Growth signals

## Rules and Guardrails

1. **Do not claim Firmable is the only platform with signals.** ZoomInfo has Copilot Signals. The correct frame is: Firmable bundles signals into the core platform, ZoomInfo charges separately for them.

2. **Do not make specific US data coverage claims.** No connect rate stats (the 22% figure is AU-sourced). No "X million US contacts" until verified.

3. **Never name-drop ZoomInfo negatively in the body.** Naming them in a subject line is fine. In the body, keep it factual — their pricing model, their architecture. Not trash-talk.

4. **Email 1 CTA is always a demo. Email 2 CTA is always a trial.** Do not swap them.

5. **PS line is required in both emails.** The early US roadmap access hook is not optional.

6. **signal_play_1 and signal_play_2 must come from Clay.** Do not hand-write signal plays per contact — they must be generated by the Clay AI column against the company's actual niche and ICP.

7. **US English throughout.** No "colour", "favour", "learnings", "whilst". American spelling and idiom.

8. **No corporate filler.** Banned: leverage, synergy, best-in-class, revolutionize, game-changer, unlock, excited to share, hope this finds you well.

9. **Target audience**: startups and mid-market. If the account is a known enterprise (1000+ employees), flag before using this sequence.

10. **Rows without signal_play columns → flag as needs_enrichment.** Run Clay columns first, then generate copy.
